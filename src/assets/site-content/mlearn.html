Overview

mLearn is a cross-platform desktop app made for Super-Fast Language Learning by immersion. Watch your favourite content in your target language, even if you're a complete beginner and you want to watch native content.

Features

  • Live word translation
  • Knows what words you know, and adjusts itself based on that
  • Integrates well with Anki
  • Advanced Flashcard Creation
  • Known word blurring
  • Power-efficient & Offline Mode
  • Supports Japanese & German
  • Streams any type of video
  • Very customizable
  • Support for Japanese Pitch-Accent
  • Support for custom languages
  • Word frequency list (like JLPT)

Supported languages

For now, Japanese and German.

Why did I make this?

This app's development was started around the Summer/Autumn of 2024. I started learning Japanese in February 2024 and was looking to immerse. But all native media were basically inaccessible to me because of my complete beginner level at that time.

I was looking for an app that would let me immerse with any content I wanted, and even offline one. Something that would have Anki integration. I found [a href="https://migaku.com/"]Migaku[/a], but it wasn't free unfortunately, nor did it allow me to jump to a whole new level of language complexity (media for natives,...).

This sparked my interest in making a complete language learning app where I could learn by immersion, even with a very basic level.

Since my level was tiny at the time and I couldn't understand 99% of the Japanese media, I invented a live word translator: it automatically appends translations of words based on their readings.

This helped me a lot, as the first piece of media I tried to watch using my app was [a href="https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2098220/"]Hunter x Hunter[/a], with Japanese subtitles and original dub.

This app got me to conversational fluency, and I learned over 1000 words with it effortlessly (I discovered this incredible number when I went through an internal database of mLearn).
